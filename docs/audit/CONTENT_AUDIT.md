# Content Audit

## Summary

The audited live pages render, but the public content still reads like a template restoration site in several high-visibility areas. The biggest production blockers are fabricated or unverifiable testimonials, unsupported response/certification/insurance claims, stock/demo imagery presented as operational proof, and city homepages that swap only the city name into the same generic copy.

The reviews page is the safest trust surface because it avoids `AggregateRating` schema and links out to Google, but sitewide testimonial modules and service stats undermine that progress. Phase 2 should prioritize the homepage because the same template feeds the apex and city homepages. Phase 6 should prioritize replacing every invented testimonial/stat/trust badge with verified proof or neutral process copy.

## Phase 2 Copy Priorities

- Replace the homepage hero with concrete, safe positioning: what Flood Doctor does, where it serves, when to call, and what happens after calling. Avoid `trusted`, `experts`, and guaranteed response unless proof-backed.
- Change primary CTA direction from `Get a Free Estimate` to emergency/service intent: `Request Service`, `Call Now`, or `Get Emergency Help`. Keep estimate language secondary for non-emergency requests.
- Remove the fake homepage testimonial module until a verified review excerpt or approved customer story is available. If no proof exists, use process reassurance instead.
- Replace stock/demo visual captions with owned/approved operational assets, or make image usage decorative and not evidence-based.
- Rewrite the `Our Track Record` section as `What to Expect` or `How We Handle Water Damage Calls`: call intake, safety/source control, inspection, extraction, drying plan, documentation, adjuster coordination.
- Replace insurance outcome claims with safe support language: documentation, photos, moisture logs, estimates, and adjuster coordination without implying carrier partnerships, direct billing availability, or maximum coverage.
- Add city homepage variation only where true: service-area neighborhoods, local building/flooding context, dispatch limitations, and local review/case proof if available.

## Phase 6 Trust Priorities

- Build a proof inventory before adding trust claims: license number, insurance proof, IICRC certificate names/numbers, Google Business Profile URL, real review excerpts, real project photos, service-area policy, and response-time policy.
- Remove or quarantine every invented testimonial: `Sarah Chen`, `James Rodriguez`, and `Daniel Park / Midwest Financial Group` unless source documentation and permission exist.
- Keep `AggregateRating` disabled unless ratings are current, sourced, and policy-safe. The current reviews-page direction is good; strengthen by linking to the canonical Google Business Profile URL instead of relying only on a share URL.
- Replace numeric service stats with verified or non-numeric proof. If `15+ years`, `24 certified technicians`, `45+ insurance partners`, `600+ projects`, `2M+ sq ft`, and `92% retained` cannot be proven, do not ship them.
- Replace certification badges/logo arrays with plain text only after verification, or with licensed official assets. Do not render fake/mislabeled Tailwind demo logos anywhere.
- Add trust copy that sounds like a real restoration company: who answers the phone, what the crew documents, how equipment placement is monitored, what the customer gets in writing, and when customers should call a plumber/insurer/utility first.
